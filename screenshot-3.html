The Drowsy Chaperone

Role
Sound Designer
Date
April 9–12, 2026
Description

The Drowsy Chaperone was my first department musical as a sound designer, and it introduced a new level of complexity compared to the plays I had worked on previously. With live vocals, musical transitions, and frequent scene changes, the production required careful planning to create a clear and cohesive sound environment before we entered performances.

I built and organized cues in QLab, collaborated with the director on the overall sound approach, and refined levels, timing, and transitions throughout tech and dress rehearsals. A major focus was balancing dialogue, singing, and accompaniment so that each element remained clear in the room without competing for attention.

This production helped me approach sound as both a storytelling tool and a driver of musical energy. It also strengthened my ability to collaborate across departments, respond quickly to rehearsal notes, and shape a show’s sound design from early planning through performance.
