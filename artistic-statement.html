Artistic Statement

To me, live sound is a way of supporting a performance as it unfolds. I’m drawn to the moments when audio helps clarify a story or deepen a mood, and I enjoy finding the balance that allows the audience to stay fully present with what is happening onstage. Working in live settings has taught me to listen carefully, stay engaged, and respond to changes with intention.

I’ve also learned that sound is shaped through collaboration. My role depends on communicating with stage management, performers, and the rest of the crew to understand what each production needs. Those relationships help me make choices that support the performance rather than distract from it.

Live sound has influenced the way I approach work under pressure. With no chance to undo anything during a show, I’ve learned to make decisions calmly and to trust my preparation. At the same time, rehearsal work has helped me appreciate how small adjustments in timing or level can make a meaningful difference in how a scene feels.

As I continue to grow, I hope to develop further as a Front of House engineer and bring clarity and consistency to performances in different spaces. My goal is to use sound in a way that supports the artist’s intentions and creates a space where audiences feel connected to the experience.
